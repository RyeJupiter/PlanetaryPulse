The Plant Health Pyramid

Advancing Eco Agriculture (AEA)'s diagnostic tool is plant sap analysis — what they call "a blood test for crops": 23 data points per sample, paired with soil geochemical testing. Their stated rationale is that they found "very little correlation between the presence of nutrients in the soil and actual absorption by the crop" — so they measure the plant directly rather than only the soil.

Brix (a sugar/photosynthesis proxy) and sap pH are read together diagnostically; low pH with moderate Brix, for example, is read as a calcium deficiency. Their framework stacks four levels:

1
Complete Photosynthesis

Correcting Mg/N/Fe/Mn/P — AEA claims this alone can drive a 3–4× Brix increase and improved fungal-pathogen resistance.

2
Complete Protein Synthesis

Correcting Mg/S/Mo/B — claimed to build insect resistance within 24–48 hours by eliminating the free amino acids that attract pests.

3
Lipid Synthesis

A waxy leaf cuticle "shield" intended to physically block airborne fungal and bacterial pathogens.

4
Secondary Metabolites

Driven by soil microbiome diversity — the level AEA ties to flavor, aroma, and nutrient density compounds.

AEA reports that on a grower's first sap test, "greater than 95%" show a manganese or iron deficiency. Correction happens through proprietary biological/mineral foliar products and soil inputs, timed to specific points in the crop's growth cycle.
