Earth Metrics

Earth Metrics are measurable dimensions of Earth function that make landscape impact on local climate visible. EarthPulse unifies these into clear, comparable stories of ecological change, especially for regeneration projects.

Think of Earth Metrics as a consistent scorecard for how land and climate systems are behaving, so comparisons stay traceable across regions and time.

Climate emerges from the land: how much light is absorbed, how much water infiltrates, how heat is stored or dissipated, and how life organizes itself. Each metric below captures a piece of that landscape-to-climate relationship.

Hydrology
Rainfall frequency, evapotranspiration, moisture dynamics, runoff proxies.
Status: Under development
Vegetation Function
Greenness and productivity (NDVI/EVI), phenology shifts, canopy trends.
Status: Under development
Thermal Regulation
Land surface temperature anomalies, heat persistence, cooling effects.
Status: Under development
Albedo and Light Absorption
Reflectance changes that influence local heat balance and stability.
Status: Under development
Atmospheric Dust and Aerosols
Aerosol optical depth and transport patterns affecting radiation and rainfall.
Status: Under development
Resilience
Variability, recovery after extremes, multi-year trend stability.
Status: Under development
