Welcome to Lighthouse Field Calisthenics Park

A grassroots, community-built movement space concept at Lighthouse Field in Santa Cruz. The project blends civic improvement, public health, local ecological learning, and a playful neighborhood identity that feels welcoming for families, kids, and everyday training.

A civil improvement project by the California Calisthenics Creepy Crawlers.

Overview

This is a local civil-improvement effort to shape a clean, durable, and beautiful outdoor movement space near West Cliff. It is designed as a public commons enhancement, not just a workout area.

Why

The goal is to create an accessible environment where movement, stewardship, and neighborhood culture reinforce each other. We want the space to feel active, friendly, and rooted in place.

What We’re Building

  • Outdoor calisthenics / bodyweight training zone.
  • Planned mulch surfacing upgrade for barefoot-friendly ground.
  • Community-built visual identity system (emblems, stickers, signage).

Ecology & Education

Educational signage is planned as laser-cut multi-layer panels with hinged reveal sections. The concept highlights local species and habitat context, including monarch butterflies, cypress, and eucalyptus, in a kid-friendly format.

Visual Identity

The design language is clean, coastal, and optimistic: sky blues, monarch orange accents, and high-contrast emblems that work across stickers, signs, and install documentation.

Current Status / Next Steps

  • Refining site layout and installation sequence.
  • Preparing mulch surfacing upgrade workflow.
  • Fabricating educational signage prototypes for field testing.
  • Growing collaboration with local volunteers and contributors.
