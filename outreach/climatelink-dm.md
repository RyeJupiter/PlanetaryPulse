## Main version (DM-length)

Hi [organizer] —

I'd love to present at an upcoming ClimateLink meetup. I've built **EarthPulse**
([earthpulse.dev](https://earthpulse.dev)), and it fits your "show what you built + the results"
format pretty exactly.

**What I built:** EarthPulse turns NASA satellite records into clear before/after evidence of
landscape regeneration — a live 3D globe + data explorer covering 8 real regeneration sites
worldwide (Al-Baydha, Loess Plateau, White Oak Pastures, and more).

**The results:** with 25 years of NASA MODIS data, you can watch regenerated land measurably green
up (NDVI) and cool down (land-surface temperature) — the regeneration "signature" shows up as a
slope change you can see from space.

**Where it's going (the part I'd most want to present):** I'm building a flood model that connects
rainfall to river flow to quantify how much water a watershed infiltrates vs. sheds — then shows
what happens if that watershed's farmland adopted the infiltration gains we already measure on real
regenerative farms. The payoff is two-sided: **lower flood peaks** and **more dry-season water for
farmers**, with **avoided FEMA flood payouts** as the funding mechanism to pay farmers to hold water
upstream. EarthPulse is the satellite measurement layer that makes it verifiable. I'm prototyping it
on the Pajaro River — the 2023 Watsonville levee breach, $300M+ in damage, an intensely agricultural
valley.

It's a builder-and-results talk with a live demo, aimed squarely at the founders, investors, and
scientists in your room. Happy to send a one-pager or hop on a quick call — what's the best way to
get on an upcoming agenda?

Thanks,
Rye · [earthpulse.dev](https://earthpulse.dev)
