Squeeze Citrus

A regenerative citrus grove near Thomasville, Georgia, run by a former Bayer pesticide researcher who put his own results through a real lab test.

Herb Young spent 36 years at Bayer doing pesticide research and development. After retiring, he bought a small citrus farm in southwest Georgia, spent about a year studying regenerative agriculture, then planted his grove. His core practice is cover cropping — a different clover/hairy-vetch-based mix seeded twice a year, winter and summer. In his words: "My goal is to grow microbes, and cover crops are the biggest feeder of microbes."

To test the results, he designed his own side-by-side comparison: he picked the same citrus varieties from his grove and from two neighboring conventional groves on the same day, juiced and froze the samples, and shipped them overnight to a USDA-certified lab in North Carolina. Three flavonoids were entirely absent from the conventional fruit but present in his. He now sells Squeeze Citrus direct-to-consumer in more than 36 states.

Caveat: this is a single grower's self-designed comparison (his grove vs. two neighbors, one harvest day) — not independently replicated or peer-reviewed. The same-day-harvest control and use of an accredited third-party lab make it more rigorous than a bare marketing claim, but it is still n=1.
