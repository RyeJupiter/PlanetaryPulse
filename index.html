Full product

Planetary Pulse is a data aggregation and visualization platform that quantifies the functional health of Earth’s living systems by tracking how water, energy, carbon, and biological structure move and regulate across the planet over time. Using satellite observations and publicly available Earth-system data, Planetary Pulse translates complex signals—across land, coasts, and oceans—into clear, trend-based indicators that show whether ecosystems are stabilizing, degrading, or regenerating relative to historical baselines and matched controls. The platform is designed to make planetary-scale processes legible to both experts and the public, supporting informed action, investment, and stewardship aimed at restoring ecological function and advancing planetary regeneration.

MVP

Planetary Pulse (MVP) is a web-based visualization tool that aggregates a small set of publicly available satellite and Earth-system datasets to display time-series trends in ecosystem function for selected regions. The MVP focuses on a limited number of core signals—such as surface temperature, vegetation productivity, and hydrologic patterns—and presents them through simple, comparative graphics that show how conditions are changing relative to historical baselines and nearby reference areas. Its purpose is to demonstrate that complex planetary processes can be translated into clear, interpretable indicators that make ecological change—and early signs of regeneration—visible and understandable.

Planetary Pulse — Why it matters

Ideological / mission value

Today, countless people and organizations are working to regenerate land, water, and ecosystems. Yet almost no one can see how these efforts alter planetary function over time, because the complexity of those impacts is immense and distributed across scales. Planetary Pulse makes this complexity visible. By translating satellite and Earth-system data into clear, longitudinal signals, the platform reveals how local actions ripple through hydrologic, thermal, carbon, and biological systems. When progress becomes visible at scale, cynicism declines and participation increases. Rather than enforcing compliance through rules or guilt, Planetary Pulse encourages learning systems—supporting understanding of Earth’s regulatory processes and reframing ecology as cooperative tuning rather than sacrifice.

Market / practical value

A market for regeneration and environmental impact already exists, but it remains fragmented and inefficient. Carbon credits, ESG reporting, consulting assessments, and bespoke dashboards attempt to fill the gap, yet many rely on self-reporting, short-term studies, or expensive consultants. Planetary Pulse provides an integrated alternative: cross-project comparison, longitudinal performance tracking, and independent verification using publicly available data—reducing reliance on one-off studies while increasing transparency. For governments, NGOs, restoration orgs, and high-end regenerative agriculture or forestry operations, it offers persistent monitoring and communicable, third-party evidence of ecological impact over years and decades.

Platform structure

Planetary Pulse is designed as a tiered platform: Tier 1 builds trust and cultural legitimacy through a high-quality public interface; Tier 2 adds professional analytics and reporting tools; Tier 3 supports verification and custom analysis for major projects and institutions.

Tier 1 — Public Intelligence (MVP)

Open dashboards, baselines, trend signals, and narrative tools.

Tier 2 — Professional Analytics

Subscriptions for NGOs, funds, developers, and agencies; advanced comparisons and reporting.

Tier 3 — Verification & Custom Analysis

Bespoke modeling, validation, and institutional-grade analyses.

Core value proposition

Transforms fragmented environmental data into shared planetary intelligence—so society can see, learn, and coordinate around living systems.
