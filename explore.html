How EarthPulse processes these signals
  • Pull every available MODIS composite for the selected point and window.
  • Apply NASA's own QA flags — drop pixels marked as snow, cloud, or not produced.
  • Take the monthly median of surviving values so single bad composites don't dominate.
  • Overlay a 12-month centred rolling mean to remove seasonal cycles.
  • Fit linear trends to the smoothed series before and after the intervention date.
What we're not doing yet

Measurements come from a single 250 m pixel at the project centroid — a patch the size of a small farm, not the entire project. A pixel at the centroid may or may not fall on the restored land, so a flat NDVI can mean the intervention worked at scale but missed this specific pixel, not that the land is unchanged. Pre-intervention windows are sometimes short (4 years at Al Baydha) which makes the fitted slope noisy, and we don't yet report confidence intervals, p-values, or an interrupted time-series significance test. We also don't compare to a climatically-matched control site. Treat the direction of the slope inflection as a suggestive signal, not a peer-reviewed attribution.
